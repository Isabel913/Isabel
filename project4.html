Project 4

Exploration

A multi-player bird shooting game using the inputs from physical controllers to control the visual outputs on the screen.

My final project landed in the categories of game, and the original thought of that was highly influenced by covid, as we are all locked in our own places, im sure everyone misses playing games and having fun with their friends.

One player would represent the bird, and the other player would represent the hunter.

In the end, the bird player would use the pressure sensor to control the birds X position, inorder to escape the bullet, then the hunter player would use the potentiomenter to control the rotation of the gun inorder to aim at the birds, they also have a button to press inorder to shoot the bullet.

Now we know how I planned to make this game work technically, let's explore more on where would the scenario take place

Lisa and Tim are planning for their beach day tomorrow.

but as we turn to next day, they are sitting on the couch, looking frustrated. We wonder, why are they still at home?

Unfortunately, they are stuck at home because it's raining outside, their beach trip is ruined.

Tim whines, "Oh no.. What do we do now?"

Lisa thought for a second and said "Oh! How about let's change today into a game day!"

She grabs the controller and her laptop.

After Lisa explained the simple instructions to Tim, they started playing the game.

Lisa wins the first round because of her familiarity in this game, and time suddenly got more interested in this game because he obviously doesn't want to lose, so he asked for another round.

They are sitting comfortably at home, enjoying the game, and totally forgot about how the weather runined their beach day.

I hope the scenario gave you more context on where this game will take place, now lets see what are the steps I took to make this game happen.

To start it off, i built the game with simple keyboard controls first, then replacing them one by one with external controllers.

In the process of replacing the controllers, I faced a lot of obstacles and challenges.

The first challenge was to map the potentiometer onto both the gun and bullet for it to rotate, while limiting the rotation to only above ground level, which I was able to solved the challenge by using push/pop matrix, translate, as well as limiting the rotation angle between half PI to one and a half PI as shown in this code.

The second challenge is to shoot the bullet when the button is pressed by the player, and making the bullet return to the gun for the next round. I had to write 3 if statements to make it happen, as shown in the picture. First, if the button is pressed, the moveUp function of bullet is activated. then, if it is activated, I gave it a speed of moving up 20 pixels per frame. lastly, for the bullet to return to the gun, i stated that when the bullet flies off the canvass, the moveup function is disabled, and it will reposition itself back to 0 for the next round.

After those worked out, I started to map the pressure sensor on to the bird's X position, for the bird player to control the speed. A few adjustments were made on the numbers to make sure the bird doesn't fly off the screen.

Also, since the pressure sensor is only a thin piece, it wouldn't be that intuitive for players to pinch that thin little piece, so I put the sensor inside a toy, for the player to press on easier. It would be much better if it was a bird toy, but i only had a bear toy that was laying around.

The last challenge is to make the score of the hunter plus only 1 point, when the bullet touches the bird. This is the issue that I am still struggling with actually, for now the code is able to detect when position of bullet is overlapping with the bird's position, but since the system is calculating the overlapping by frame, so im still struggling with how to make it add only one point.

But the score for the bird works fine, as the bullet flies off the canvass with out touching the bird, the score of bird would +1, as it means that the bird has escaped a round.

After assembling and connecting all the external controllers to my photon board, I started to package the game into a box, so it will be more portable when I want to take it around.

In the process of completing this game, I realized that feather and my project are similar in a way, not only the violence in game, but also the way we assembled our controllers, as well as how we plan to use them in the game, so we spent time together to disscuss with each other about the mistakes we made and the success we achieved, to help each other's projects grow along the way.
